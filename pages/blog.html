Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about sustainable agriculture and our innovative solutions.

Black Soldier Fly larvae are the larval stage of the Hermetia illucens fly. They are incredibly efficient at converting organic waste into high-quality protein and nutrients. These larvae can process food waste, agricultural residues, and other organic materials, transforming them into valuable products like protein meal, oil, and organic fertilizer (frass).
Organic frass offers several advantages over synthetic fertilizers. It provides slow-release nutrients, improves soil structure, enhances water retention, and promotes beneficial microbial activity. Unlike chemical fertilizers, frass doesn't cause soil acidification or groundwater contamination, making it a sustainable long-term solution for soil health.
Yes, insect protein from Black Soldier Fly larvae is safe when properly processed. It's rich in essential amino acids, contains beneficial fatty acids, and has been approved by various food safety authorities worldwide. Many countries already include insect protein in animal feed, and it's increasingly being accepted for human consumption as well.
Our BSF farming methods have a significantly lower environmental footprint compared to traditional livestock farming. They require 95% less land, 99% less water, and produce 99% fewer greenhouse gas emissions. Additionally, by converting organic waste into valuable products, we're contributing to a circular economy and reducing landfill waste.
Farmers can start by contacting us through our website or WhatsApp. We provide consultation services to help determine the best products for specific farming needs. We also offer guidance on application rates, timing, and best practices. Our team provides ongoing support to ensure optimal results and customer satisfaction.
